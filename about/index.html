About Shu Uesugi

Hi, I'm Shu Uesugi. I code in Ruby, design in Web Inspector, and write in English and Japanese. I work at EdSurge in San Francisco Bay Area.

I was born in Osaka, Japan on January 26, 1988 to Masako Uesugi and Kazuya Uesugi and have a younger brother, Shinsaku Uesugi.

Professional

I love studying hard problems, even though I'm not exactly the best at solving them. Currently, I'm interested in the following hard problems in the startup world: (1) software architecture, (2) product design, and (3) growth strategies. I've taken some baby steps in mastering these areas:

  • I obtained a CS degree from Carnegie Mellon University; I worked at Palantir Technologies as a software engineer upon graduation.
  • I obtained a HCI masters degree, also from Carnegie Mellon University. After Palantir, I worked at Quora as a product designer.
  • When I was a designer at Quora, I focused on user growth. At EdSurge, I also focus on user growth from an engineering point of view.

In 2013, I'll strive to become a specialist in each of the above fields. I challenge myself to:

  • Release and maintain a Ruby library - probably an API wrapper of some sort - and make my empty Github profile more exciting.
  • Teach product design to a friend of mine who aspires to become a designer. I learned in college that teaching is the best way to learn.
  • Grow EdSurge.

Places

I don't have any favorite hobbies, but I can name my favorite cities. They're mostly the places I've lived in. In the U.S.:

  • Potomac, Maryland is the only place I call home. I lived here when I first moved to the U.S. (2000-2006). Probably one of the best places on earth to raise a kid.
  • Georgetown, Washington D.C. will always be my favorite hang-out/shopping place. Beautiful waterfront views of the Potomac River.
  • Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania is where I lived in college. I think I know the city inside out. My favorite neighborhoods were Squirrel Hill, East Liberty, and Mount Washington.
  • Cupertino, California is the first Silicon Valley city I lived in, when I interned at Apple. I miss the days I biked around the town.
  • Menlo Park, California is my current base. I love the downtown.

In Japan:

  • Yokohama, Kanagawa is where I lived during my elementary school years, and also during the Spring/Summer of 2012. Not much night life, but still a beautiful city with a lot going on. Also very close to Chigasaki, my favorite beachtown, and Tamachi, my favorite residential area in Tokyo.
  • Shibuya, Tokyo - my default go-out place. Startup scenes are also emerging in Shibuya and its neighboring cities Roppongi and Ebisu.
  • Tanabe, Wakayama is where my grandparents live. My best childhood memories are kept in this old town.
  • Chita, Aichi is my favorite beachtown in Central Japan. The food is delicious. Also close to my favorite airport (NGO).
  • Kyoto City, Kyoto is where I dream of living. Kyoto is the old capital of Japan and an incredibly popular place for travelers.
