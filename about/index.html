About Shu Uesugi

Professional

I love studying hard problems, even though I'm not exactly the best at solving them. I'm especially interested in the following problems in the startup world: (1) software architecture, (2) product design, and (3) growth strategies.

I've taken some baby steps in mastering these areas:

  • I obtained a CS degree from Carnegie Mellon University; I worked at Palantir Technologies as a software engineer upon graduation.
  • I obtained a HCI masters degree, also from Carnegie Mellon University. After Palantir, I worked at Quora as a product designer.
  • When I was a designer at Quora, I focused on user growth. At EdSurge, I also focus on user growth from an engineering point of view.

In 2013, I'll strive to become a specialist in each of the above fields. I challenge myself to:

  • Release and maintain a Ruby library - probably an API wrapper of some sort - and make my empty Github profile more exciting.
  • Teach product design to a friend of mine who aspires to become a designer. I learned in college that teaching is the best way to learn.
  • Grow EdSurge.

Philosophy

In the first quarter century of my life, I learned the following three lessons:

First, your family is the most important piece of your life. My logo illustrates my immediate family: my mother is the left block, my father is the bottom block, my brother is the bottom right block, and I"m the top right block. The logo can also be read as “S” (my first initial), “U” (my last initial), and ä¸Š (the first Japanese character of my last name).

Second, while entrepreneurship is about discovering problems and disrupting the status quo, you must not forget to appreciate things around you, even if they're far from being perfect. Be grateful for someone else's work before coming up with an improvement. Be thankful to people in your comfort zone before stepping out of it.

Third, for each field you consider to be your specialities, define what it means to you, in your own words. For example,

  • Teaching is a series of two step processes. Let's say you want to teach X and Y to someone. First, make X seem easy and worth mastering. Second, have your student do X from start to finish on their own. Then repeat for Y.
  • Coding is about changes. Change the code to make it easier to change in the future.
  • Design is about decisions. Decide on a set of hidden rules - rules that users will never notice.

Places

My favorite cities in the U.S.:

  • Potomac, Maryland is the only place I call home. I lived here when I first moved to the U.S. (2000-2006). Probably one of the best places on earth to raise a kid.
  • Georgetown, Washington D.C. will always be my favorite hang-out/shopping place. Beautiful waterfront views of the Potomac River.
  • Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania is where I lived in college. I know the city inside out. My favorite neighborhoods were Squirrel Hill, East Liberty, and Mount Washington.
  • Cupertino, California is the first Silicon Valley city I lived in for my Apple internship. I miss hanging out in the Apple campus and spotting Steve Jobs.
  • Menlo Park, California is my current base. I love running on Seminary Dr.

My favorite cities in Japan:

  • Yokohama, Kanagawa is where I lived during my elementary school years, and also during the Spring/Summer of 2012. Not much night life, but still a beautiful city with a lot going on. Also very close to Chigasaki, my favorite beachtown.
  • Shibuya, Tokyo - my default hang-out place in Tokyo. Startup scenes are also emerging in Shibuya and its neighboring cities Roppongi and Ebisu.
  • Tanabe, Wakayama is a rural town where my grandparents live. Lots of childhood memories.
  • Chita, Aichi is my favorite beachtown in Central Japan. Also close to my favorite airport (NGO).
  • Kyoto City, Kyoto is where I dream of living. Kyoto is the old capital of Japan. Words cannot describe how elegant this city is.
